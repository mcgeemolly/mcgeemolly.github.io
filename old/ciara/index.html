Academic Projects

Here are some examples of my work during my undergraduate studies. Click on the links in the description to read about the projects in more detail.

CMS 340K: Torches of Freedom Campaign

In the course Communication and Social Change, I researched how the Social Marketing Theory could be used to explain the “Torches of Freedom," one of the first public relations campaigns. This project became an example for students to reference in the course the following semesters.
Watch my research project video here.

COM 301: Study Tips and Email Etiquette Guides

As a member of the teaching team (title: Undergraduate Learning Assitant) for the course COM 301, we were constantly looking to increase the number of resources for students (the majority of which were incoming freshman). We created a Study Tips Guide based on the different learning styles of students as well as a Email Etiquette Guide for writing professional emails.
Read the Study Tips Guide here.
Read the Email Etiquette Guide here.

CMS 372K: A Guide to Planning a Successful Academic Lecture

In the course Organizational Communication, my partner and I created a guide for organizers of academic lectures to reference for strategies on how to increase audience engagement. We created the recommendations through the lenses of event planning and communication because both factors contribute to the execution of a successful lecture.
Read my guide here.

CMS 353S: Social Media Client Strategic Plan

In the course Social Media and Organizations, my partner and I developed an extensive plan for the operation of Moody College's Office of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion social media accounts including methods for measuring engagement, identifying relevant stakeholders, and styling content such as messaging and visual guidelines.
Read my social media plan here.

I 302: Delivery Demand Affects Business Plans

In the course Academic Success at a Digital University, I completed an individual research project on how businesses have adapted their technology and practices to address the demand for delivery services during COVID-19.
Watch my research project video here.
